HIS BIOGRAPHY

Hwan Wilson Yang (born December 02, 1980) is a distinguished energy executive and the Chief Executive Officer of Drillnex Offshore Services, bringing over two decades of international experience in the oil and gas industry. He was born in Busan, South Korea, and moved to Finland at the age of two, where he was raised in Stockholm. This multicultural upbringing played a key role in shaping his global perspective and adaptability in international business environments.

Hwan developed an early interest in engineering, energy systems, and global business, which would later define his professional journey. He began his academic career at Helsinki University of Technology (now part of Aalto University), enrolling in September 1995 and graduating in June 2000 with a Bachelor's degree in Petroleum Engineering. Committed to advancing his expertise, he pursued a Master's degree at Stanford University in the United States from September 2001 to June 2003, gaining advanced knowledge in energy systems, reservoir engineering, and strategic management.

Following his education, Hwan built a strong professional foundation by working with some of the world's most respected energy companies. His early career included roles at Schlumberger, where he gained hands-on experience in drilling and reservoir services, and Halliburton, where he further developed his technical and operational expertise in complex offshore environments. He later joined Saudi Aramco, one of the largest energy companies globally, where he contributed to major projects across the Middle East, strengthening his leadership capabilities and global industry insight.

Through these roles, Hwan developed deep expertise in offshore drilling operations, project execution, and cross-cultural leadership, managing large-scale projects in challenging environments. His ability to integrate technical precision with strategic thinking quickly distinguished him as a leader in the field.
